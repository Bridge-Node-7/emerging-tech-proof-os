# IP Boundary

Emerging Tech Proof OS is an open public governance layer and proof standard.

It is designed to make review discipline visible without forcing proprietary work into the public record.

## What Belongs in Public Instances

Public instances may include:

- templates
- workflows
- evidence card formats
- decision brief formats
- AI governance gate formats
- synthetic examples
- public-safe examples
- documentation explaining how the structure works
- this repository's doctrine, limitations, and operating principles

## What Does Not Belong in Public Instances

Public instances should not include:

- proprietary technology claims
- commercial models
- patent strategy
- private scoring logic
- sensitive supply chain intelligence
- client data
- internal strategy
- classified, controlled, or restricted information
- real credentials, private keys, internal hostnames, or operational vulnerabilities
- non-public incident details
- personal data

## Private Work

Evidence cards, briefs, or technology claims involving proprietary or restricted content should be maintained in private repositories or released only in redacted, approved form under appropriate terms.

## Open Method, Protected Content

The governance methodology is open for adoption and improvement.

Specific protected content remains under the control of its originators.

Public proof should show discipline. Private systems should protect the engine.
