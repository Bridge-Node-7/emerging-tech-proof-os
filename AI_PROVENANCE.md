# AI Provenance and Human Responsibility

AI may assist with drafting, organizing, summarizing, formatting, and consistency checking.

AI does not replace evidence, judgment, validation, or responsibility.

## Requirements for AI-Assisted Content

Every artifact that includes meaningful AI assistance should record:

- which parts were AI-drafted, AI-summarized, or AI-organized
- model used and high-level prompt summary when relevant
- observed facts vs analysis or inference
- confidence level with justification
- validation required
- named or role-identified human reviewer
- limitations and known uncertainty

## Human Judgment Gate

No AI-assisted content should be merged, published, or described as validated until a human reviewer has reviewed the artifact, accepted responsibility, and documented the review status.

Automation can check structure and safety patterns.

It cannot replace human accountability.

## Public Release Rule

If AI-assisted content may affect policy, funding, security, safety, operations, or public claims, it should complete an AI governance gate before release.

Use `templates/ai-governance-gate.md`.

## Authorship and Responsibility

This repository treats human review, selection, arrangement, editing, and approval as essential.

AI-generated or AI-assisted text must not be treated as independently authoritative.

Evidence over hype. Human judgment is final.
